**1\. Reverse Number**
======================

**Input:**

12345

**Output:**

54321

**Explanation:** Reverse the digits of the given number using % (modulus) and / (division) operators.

**2\. Palindrome Number**
=========================

**Input:**

121

**Output:**

Palindrome

**Explanation:** A palindrome number remains the same when its digits are reversed. Example: 121 → 121

**3\. Armstrong Number**
========================

**Input:**

153

**Output:**

Armstrong Number

**Explanation:** An Armstrong number is equal to the sum of each digit raised to the power of the total number of digits.

Example:

153

\= 1³ + 5³ + 3³

\= 1 + 125 + 27

\= 153

**4\. Prime Number**
====================

**Input:**

29

**Output:**

Prime Number

**Explanation:** A prime number has exactly two factors: 1 and itself.

Factors of 29:

1, 29

**5\. Fibonacci Series**
========================

**Input:**

7

**Output:**

0 1 1 2 3 5 8

**Explanation:** Each number is the sum of the previous two numbers.

0

1

0+1 = 1

1+1 = 2

1+2 = 3

2+3 = 5

3+5 = 8

**6\. Factorial**
=================

**Input:**

5

**Output:**

120

**Explanation:** Factorial of a number is the product of all positive integers from 1 to the given number.

5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120

**7\. Swap Two Numbers**

10

**8\. GCD (Greatest Common Divisor)**
=====================================

**Input:**

12

18

**Output:**

6

**Explanation:** The GCD is the largest number that divides both numbers without leaving a remainder.

Factors:

12 → 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

18 → 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18

Greatest Common Factor = 6

**9\. LCM (Least Common Multiple)**
===================================

**Input:**

12

18

**Output:**

36

**Explanation:** The LCM is the smallest positive number that is divisible by both numbers.

Multiples:

12 → 12, 24, 36, 48...

18 → 18, 36, 54...

LCM = 36

**12\. Decimal to Binary**
==========================

**Input:**

13

**Output:**

1101

**Explanation:** Convert the decimal number into its binary equivalent by repeatedly dividing by 2 and collecting the remainders.

13 ÷ 2 = 6 remainder 1

6 ÷ 2 = 3 remainder 0

3 ÷ 2 = 1 remainder 1

1 ÷ 2 = 0 remainder 1

Read from bottom to top:

1101

**13\. Binary to Decimal**
==========================

**Input:**

1101

**Output:**

13

**Explanation:** Multiply each binary digit by the corresponding power of 2 and add the results.

1101

1 × 2³ = 8

1 × 2² = 4

0 × 2¹ = 0

1 × 2⁰ = 1

8 + 4 + 0 + 1 = 13

**14\. Strong Number**
======================

**Input:**

145

**Output:**

Strong Number

**Explanation:** A Strong Number is equal to the sum of the factorials of its digits.

1! + 4! + 5!

\= 1 + 24 + 120

\= 145

**15\. Perfect Number**
=======================

**Input:**

28

**Output:**

Perfect Number

**Explanation:** A Perfect Number is equal to the sum of its proper divisors (excluding the number itself).

Factors of 28:

1, 2, 4, 7, 14

1 + 2 + 4 + 7 + 14 = 28

**16\. Happy Number**
=====================

**Input:**

19

**Output:**

Happy Number

**Explanation:** Replace the number with the sum of the squares of its digits repeatedly until it becomes 1 (Happy Number) or enters a loop (Not Happy).

19

1² + 9² = 82

8² + 2² = 68

6² + 8² = 100

1² + 0² + 0² = 1

Happy Number

**17\. Automorphic Number**
===========================

**Input:**

25

**Output:**

Automorphic Number

**Explanation:** An Automorphic Number is a number whose square ends with the same number.

25² = 625

625 ends with 25

Therefore,

25 is an Automorphic Number.

**18\. Harshad Number**
=======================

**Input:**

18

**Output:**

Harshad Number

**Explanation:** A Harshad Number is divisible by the sum of its digits.

1 + 8 = 9

18 ÷ 9 = 2

No remainder

Therefore,

18 is a Harshad Number.

**19\. Spy Number**
===================

**Input:**

123

**Output:**

Spy Number

**Explanation:** A Spy Number is a number in which the sum of its digits is equal to the product of its digits.

1 + 2 + 3 = 6

1 × 2 × 3 = 6

Since both are equal,

123 is a Spy Number.

**20\. Neon Number**
====================

**Input:**

9

**Output:**

Neon Number

**Explanation:** A Neon Number is a number where the sum of the digits of its square is equal to the original number.

9² = 81

8 + 1 = 9

Therefore,

9 is a Neon Number.
